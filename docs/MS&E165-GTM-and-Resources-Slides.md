## 5. Go-To-Market Strategy

### Slide: Launch Plan (Channels + Timeline)

**Title:** Launch Plan

**Channels**

| Channel | Role |
|--------|------|
| **In-product (PearPad)** | Primary: PearNavigator ships as a system feature; first touch for 35M PearPad users, ~50% creatives. No separate install = maximum reach. |
| **Pear ecosystem (PearCloud, Pear Desk)** | Cross-sell: Promoted in creative workflows (e.g., “Use PearNavigator on PearPad for this task”). Drives multi-device engagement. |
| **Creative community & education** | Awareness: Short demos and “how I use PearNavigator” content with design influencers and bootcamps (e.g., Figma-focused). Builds credibility with Visual Designers. |
| **Enterprise / teams** | Expansion: Pitch to design leads and L&D for onboarding and reskilling; team adoption drives retention and upsell to PearCreate+. |

**Timeline (align with your Execution Plan):** Alpha (50+ power users, Months 1–4) → Beta (broader rollout, Weeks 5–8) → Model tuning & automation (Months 5–6) → General availability (Month 6+).

### Slide: Target Metrics

**Title:** Target Metrics

| Phase | Metric | Target | Why it matters |
|-------|--------|--------|----------------|
| **Alpha** | Suggestion acceptance | >85% | Validates that guidance is relevant and actionable. |
| **Beta** | 2-week retention | >60% | Confirms habit formation and ongoing value. |
| **Launch** | 30-day adoption (eligible creatives) | >20% | Proves launch channel and positioning. |
| **Post-launch** | Task completion with Navigator help | Track % sessions where user completes intended task with Navigator; goal: improve vs. baseline. |
| **Post-launch** | Abandonment to external help | Reduce % of sessions where user leaves for Google/videos. |

---

### Slide: Measuring Impact Post-Launch

**Title:** Measuring Impact Post-Launch

**Strategy:** Use leading indicators (usage, acceptance) and outcome indicators (completion, retention, NPS) to iterate and report success.

| Category | Metrics | How we use them |
|----------|---------|------------------|
| **Adoption** | % eligible creatives who try PearNavigator in first week; avg # help requests per user per week | Track reach and engagement; optimize in-product discovery. |
| **Execution** | % sessions where user completes intended task with Navigator; % steps completed on first attempt | Measure guidance quality and UX; prioritize model and overlay improvements. |
| **Outcomes** | % sessions where user abandons to external help; % users who later complete same task without Navigator | Prove reduction in context-switching and sustained learning. |
| **Business** | PearPad creative engagement (time in supported apps); NPS (Navigator users); conversion to PearCreate+ | Link product success to ecosystem and revenue (per your Business Impact slide). |

*Optional one-liner:* “We review these weekly post-launch and tie product roadmap and model retraining to the execution and outcome metrics.”

## 7. Required Resources

### Slide: Required Resources (Ask)

**Title:** Required Resources

**Funding**

- **What:** Budget for cloud/API (vision + LLM), device-fleet testing, and a small alpha/beta incentive fund (e.g., gift cards for power users).
- **How it supports launch:** Ensures we can run at scale during alpha/beta without latency or cost caps, and recruit enough alpha testers for statistically meaningful validation of acceptance and retention targets.

**Tools**

- **What:** Vision/LLM APIs (or internal models), OCR (e.g., Tesseract), device lab or device-cloud access for PearPad + creative apps (Figma, Photoshop, etc.), and analytics (in-app events, A/B if needed).
- **How it supports launch:** Reliable inference and overlay pipeline plus real-device testing ensure the product works in the exact environment users have; analytics let us measure adoption, execution, and outcome metrics and tune the experience.

**Team roles**

- **What:** Product (roadmap, metrics, alpha/beta design); Engineering (edge/cloud ML, overlay, integrations); Design (UX for overlay and flows); optionally Research (user interviews, usability).
- **How it supports launch:** Clear ownership for build, quality, and user feedback so we hit alpha/beta milestones and can iterate on acceptance and retention before full launch.

**Partnerships**

- **What:** Relationships with creative-app ecosystems (e.g., Figma, Adobe) for early access, betas, or co-marketing; design communities and bootcamps for alpha recruitment and case studies.
- **How it supports launch:** Improves credibility with Visual Designers, speeds alpha/beta recruitment, and creates distribution and storytelling for post-launch adoption and enterprise expansion.
